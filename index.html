What We Do
Research

We study how spin waves (magnons) interact with phonons, photons, and electric currents in the microwave regime, to build next-generation devices for quantum information, neuromorphic computing, and spin-based signal processing.

01

Magnon–Phonon Coupling

Coherent interaction between magnons and phonons in engineered platforms. Vibrational modes mediate long-range magnon coupling and energy transport.

Phys. Rev. X 12, 011060 (2022) Phys. Rev. B 101, 060407 (2020) Phys. Rev. Applied 20, 014046 (2023)
02

Spin-Wave Logic & Neuromorphic Devices

Spin-wave devices that perform logic operations and emulate neural functions using nonlinear magnon interactions.

Nat. Commun. 15, 7302 (2024) Phys. Rev. Applied 11, 034065 (2019)
03

Quantum Magnonics

Hybrid quantum systems and information transfer at GHz frequencies, toward quantum communication and quantum sensing.

Phys. Rev. X 12, 011060 (2022)
04

Spin-Caloritronics

Spin and heat interactions — thermally driven spin currents, nonlocal heat transfer via magnons and phonons, energy-efficient spin-based devices.

Phys. Rev. B 103, 174432 (2021) Phys. Rev. Lett. 117, 107202 (2016)

Ongoing Projects

Glocal R&D — Magnon-Superconductor Quantum Transduction

2025 – 2028

Building a regional Open-Lab infrastructure for cryogenic quantum transduction between superconducting qubits and magnonic systems, including magnon squeezed state generation.

ITRC Program — Magnon-Based Quantum Sensing

2024 – 2031

Part of the Information Technology Research Center program at CBNU, developing magnon-based device platforms for next-generation quantum sensing technologies.

Sejong Science Fellowship

2021 – 2026

Frontier research on quantum-like magnon dynamics, spin-phonon interactions, and low-dissipation information transport using engineered phononic structures.
